Help with the map

GPS delay

The information shown on maps is based on the GPS data received from tags which is usually subject to delays of around 10 to 15 minutes.

Delays vary depending on network coverage and how often tags upload GPS data.

Use timestamps to find out when and how long a person on probation visited a location.

Locations of interest

As a person on probation enters or leaves areas a location of interest this movement is recorded in their location history and on their map.

Locations of interest have defined boundaries which reflect the real-world and include:

  • exclusion and inclusion zones
  • home and other addresses - for example, family, friend and parental
  • places of interest - for example, pubs
  • required locations - for example, places of work and training providers

View full list of locations of interest

Confidence circles

The centre of a circle shows a person on probation’s most likely location.

Circle size reflects the accuracy of GPS data and our level of confidence about the person on probation’s exact location. For example:

  • smaller circles = more confidence about a location
  • larger circles = less confidence about a location

Various factors affect the accuracy of GPS data and the size of confidence circles including:

  • building density
  • GPS drift
  • GPS satellite availability
  • signal obstruction

View full list of factors

Lines and arrows

The lines on maps show the likely path a person on probation took between locations and do not represent a confirmed route.

They only estimate and give a general sense of the person on probation's movement over time.

The arrows on lines show a person on probation's direction of travel.
